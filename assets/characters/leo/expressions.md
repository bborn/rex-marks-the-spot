# Leo Bornsztein - Key Expressions

## Overview
Leo provides emotional authenticity as the youngest character. His expressions are bigger, more immediate, and less filtered than the adults. He wears his heart on his sleeve.

---

## Primary Expressions

### 1. Wonder/Amazement
**Scene Usage**: Seeing dinosaurs, magic, Jurassic world
**Key Features**:
- Eyes HUGE (almost circular)
- Mouth forms perfect "O"
- Eyebrows high and rounded
- Head tilted back to look up
- Hands may come up toward face

**Reference Scenes**: First sight of Jetplane, Jurassic landscape reveal

---

### 2. Scared/Hiding
**Scene Usage**: T-Rex encounters, dangerous moments
**Key Features**:
- Eyes wide with tears forming at corners
- Lower lip trembling
- Eyebrows pinched together and up
- Body curled, making himself small
- Often peeks through fingers or from behind Mia

**Reference Scenes**: T-Rex roar, dark forest, any threat

---

### 3. Brave Moment
**Scene Usage**: Key heroic beats, standing up despite fear
**Key Features**:
- Eyes still show fear but jaw is set
- Fists clenched at sides
- Chest puffed out (slightly comical given his size)
- Eyebrows determined
- "I can do this" energy despite shaking

**Reference Scenes**: Helping save parents, protecting Jetplane

---

### 4. Pure Joy
**Scene Usage**: Playing with Jetplane, reunion with parents
**Key Features**:
- Gap-toothed grin (full teeth showing)
- Eyes crinkled shut or sparkling
- Cheeks pushed up high
- Often involves bouncing or jumping
- Arms may go up in excitement

**Reference Scenes**: Reuniting with mom and dad, playing with Jetplane

---

### 5. Missing Parents/Sad
**Scene Usage**: Realizing parents are gone, quiet worried moments
**Key Features**:
- Eyes downcast, watery
- Lower lip pushed out
- Eyebrows pinched in middle
- Shoulders slumped
- May be hugging toy dinosaur

**Reference Scenes**: "Where's mommy and daddy?"

---

### 6. Curious/Investigating
**Scene Usage**: Exploring, asking questions
**Key Features**:
- Head tilted to side
- One eye slightly squinted
- Finger pointing or near mouth
- Eyebrows asymmetrical
- Leaning toward object of interest

**Reference Scenes**: Examining Jurassic plants, meeting new creatures

---

### 7. Defiant/Stubborn
**Scene Usage**: "No, I wanna help!" moments
**Key Features**:
- Puffed cheeks
- Furrowed brow
- Arms crossed or fists on hips
- Chin jutted forward
- Feet planted wide

**Reference Scenes**: Insisting on joining the rescue, refusing to stay behind

---

## Secondary Expressions

### 8. Sleepy/Tired
- Half-lidded eyes
- Yawning or rubbing eyes
- Head drooping
- Used in opening/closing scenes

### 9. Grossed Out
- Tongue out
- Nose scrunched
- Eyes squinted
- One eye open more than other
- Used for Jetplane's colorful farts

### 10. Giggling/Mischievous
- Hand over mouth
- Shoulders bouncing
- Eyes sparkling
- Barely contained laughter
- Used for funny moments with Jetplane

---

## Expression Transitions

### Scared → Brave
Leo's character arc in miniature:
1. Eyes dart to Mia for reassurance
2. Deep breath (chest rises)
3. Eyes narrow with determination
4. Small nod to self

### Wonder → Fear
Quick transition for dinosaur moments:
1. Amazement holds for beat
2. Realization dawns (eyes change first)
3. "Oh no" moment before full fear

---

## Unique Leo Elements

### The Gap-Tooth
- Visible in smiles, certain phonemes
- Adds charm and age-accuracy
- Slight whistle on "S" sounds (animation note)

### The Tears
- Leo tears up easily (authentic 5-year-old)
- Tears should well but not always fall
- Adds emotional weight without being melodramatic

### The Pout
- Default "things aren't going my way" face
- Lower lip prominent
- Used for comic relief and genuine emotion

---

## Mouth Shapes (Phonemes)
For dialogue (remember the gap tooth):
- A/I - open mouth, gap visible
- O/U - rounded lips
- E - wide smile, gap very visible
- M/B/P - closed lips
- S sounds - slight gap whistle effect
- TH - tongue visible through gap

---

## Notes for Animators
- Leo's emotions change FAST - he's 5, no emotional buffer
- His default is curious/alert, ready for next thing
- When scared, his bravery comes from love (for family, for Jetplane)
- Joy is full-body - never just facial
- Tears are always close to the surface but he tries to be brave
- Physical comedy opportunities: running, stumbling, reactions
- He often mimics Mia's expressions a beat later
