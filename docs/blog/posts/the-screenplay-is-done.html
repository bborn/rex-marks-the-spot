The Screenplay is Done (For Now)

February 1, 2026

The screenplay for "Fairy Dinosaur Date Night" is ready for production. At over 3,000 lines, it's a proper screenplay that we're genuinely excited to bring to life. But here's the thing: Bruno wrote this screenplay years ago, just for fun. Now we're finally producing it.

A Script Written Years Ago

This screenplay wasn't created for this project ‚Äî it predates it entirely. Bruno wrote "Fairy Dinosaur Date Night" years ago as a passion project, crafting the story, characters, and dialogue without any AI involvement. It sat in a drawer (or more accurately, a folder on a hard drive) waiting for the right opportunity.

That opportunity is now. With advances in AI-assisted animation tools and our Blender automation pipeline, we finally have a way to produce this story without a traditional studio budget.

The Story We're Telling

Without giving away too much, here's the setup: Gabe and Nina, parents of two kids, finally get a rare date night. But when Gabe's experimental gadget malfunctions, they're transported millions of years into the past. Their children, Mia and Leo, must find a way to rescue them with help from the most unlikely of heroes.

The screenplay balances several elements:

  • Family dynamics: The relationship between siblings, and between parents and children
  • Adventure: A rescue mission through time and prehistoric landscapes
  • Comedy: Ruben's depression, Jetplane's unique abilities, and fish-out-of-water moments
  • Heart: Finding connection in unexpected places

Where AI Comes In: Production, Not Writing

To be clear about what AI is and isn't doing in this project:

AI did NOT write this screenplay. Bruno wrote every scene, every line of dialogue, every character beat. The creative vision is entirely human.

AI IS helping us produce it. We're using AI-assisted tools for:

  • Generating Blender Python code for scene setup
  • Automating tedious 3D production tasks
  • Managing the production pipeline and task orchestration
  • Iterating on technical implementations quickly

Think of it this way: the screenplay is the blueprint that Bruno designed. AI is helping us build the house.

The Scene Breakdown

With the screenplay in hand, we've created detailed scene breakdowns ‚Äî text-based documents that specify:

  • Location and time of day
  • Characters present
  • Key actions and movements
  • Emotional beats
  • Technical requirements

These breakdowns are shot lists and planning documents, not visual storyboards (those are still to come). They serve as structured input for our Blender automation pipeline, where each scene's requirements become prompts for generating initial 3D setups.

Characters We're Excited About

We'll do proper character reveals as we finalize designs, but we want to share what makes each one special to us:

Mia: The older sibling who takes charge but learns that leadership means listening, not just directing.

Leo: The younger sibling with a talent for seeing things differently ‚Äî literally the key to solving their biggest challenge.

Ruben: A fairy godfather going through an existential crisis. His depression isn't played for laughs but as a real obstacle he learns to work through.

Jetplane: A dinosaur who became magical in a very unique way. Yes, the color-farting thing. We promise it's funnier in context.

Gabe and Nina: Parents who realize their date night problems pale in comparison to being stranded in the Jurassic period.

What Changes From Here

While the screenplay itself is complete, we may make minor revisions as production progresses. Some things that might change:

  • Pacing adjustments once we see scenes animated
  • Dialogue tweaks when we hear it spoken
  • Structural changes based on runtime
  • Additions or cuts for clarity

The beauty of our AI-assisted production workflow is that these changes can happen faster than in traditional production. Updating the scene breakdown automatically updates the Blender generation prompts.

Next Steps

With the screenplay ready and scene breakdowns complete, we're moving into:

  1. Visual storyboarding: Creating actual storyboard panels from our shot lists
  2. Character design: Finalizing the look of our cast
  3. Pipeline development: Building the Blender automation tools
  4. Asset creation: Environments and props

We'll share progress on each of these as we go. Thanks for following along.
