Our Philosophy

We believe AI is a tool, not a replacement for human creativity. Every decision in this film goes through human judgment. AI helps us work faster, explore more options, and handle tedious tasks â€” but the creative vision remains human.

We're also committed to transparency. The AI-assisted filmmaking space is full of hype and misleading claims. We want to show what's actually possible: the wins, the failures, and the messy middle ground.

Our Production Pipeline

Here's how a scene goes from concept to render in our workflow:

1

Script & Scene Description

Bruno wrote the screenplay years ago. We break it down into detailed text-based scene descriptions for production. Story and creative decisions were entirely human-made.

2

Shot Planning

Each scene gets broken into shots with camera angles, movements, and timing. This is traditional filmmaking craft â€” AI doesn't replace the eye of a director.

3

Blender Scene Generation

Our LLM-Blender pipeline generates initial scene setups from descriptions. This gives us a starting point to iterate from, rather than building from scratch.

4

Validation & Iteration

We review renders against our intent. If something's off, we either adjust manually or feed corrections back through the pipeline. Multiple passes are normal.

5

Polish & Final Render

Once scenes are structurally correct, we add the finishing touches that require human judgment: timing tweaks, emotional beats, visual details that sell the moment.

What AI Does (and Doesn't Do)

AI Handles:

  • Initial scene setup and layout
  • Code generation for Blender scripting
  • Iteration on technical parameters
  • Consistency checking across scenes
  • Documentation and organization
  • Rapid prototyping and exploration

Humans Handle:

  • Story and character decisions
  • Emotional beats and timing
  • Creative direction and vision
  • Quality assessment and approval
  • Voice and performance direction
  • Final artistic judgment

Tools We Use

Blender

Our primary 3D animation tool. It's open-source, powerful, and has an excellent Python API that makes automation possible.

Claude

Our AI assistant for code generation, writing assistance, and pipeline orchestration. We use it through Claude Code for seamless development workflows.

Python

The glue that connects everything. Our automation scripts, Blender integration, and validation tools are all Python-based.

Git

Version control for everything â€” screenplay, documentation, code, and assets. Every change is tracked and reversible.

Challenges We Face

We're not going to pretend this is easy. Here are real challenges we're working through:

The Semantic Gap

Translating artistic intent into precise technical instructions is hard. "Make it feel more ominous" doesn't map directly to render settings.

Iteration Speed

Even with automation, render times add up. Finding the balance between quality and iteration speed is an ongoing challenge.

Consistency

Ensuring characters look the same across hundreds of shots requires careful asset management and validation.

The Uncanny Valley

AI-generated elements can feel "off" in subtle ways. Human review is essential for catching these issues.
