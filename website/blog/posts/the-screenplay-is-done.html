The Screenplay is Done (For Now)

February 1, 2026

We've completed the first draft of "Fairy Dinosaur Date Night." At over 3,000 lines, it's a proper screenplay that we're genuinely excited about. But as anyone who's worked in film knows, "done" is always temporary in creative work.

The Story We're Telling

Without giving away too much, here's the setup: Gabe and Nina, parents of two kids, finally get a rare date night. But when Gabe's experimental gadget malfunctions, they're transported millions of years into the past. Their children, Mia and Leo, must find a way to rescue them with help from the most unlikely of heroes.

The screenplay balances several elements:

  • Family dynamics: The relationship between siblings, and between parents and children
  • Adventure: A rescue mission through time and prehistoric landscapes
  • Comedy: Ruben's depression, Jetplane's unique abilities, and fish-out-of-water moments
  • Heart: Finding connection in unexpected places

Writing with AI Assistance

We used AI throughout the writing process, and we want to be transparent about how. Here's what worked and what didn't.

What Worked

Brainstorming and iteration: When we had a scene that felt flat, we could quickly generate alternatives and find the right direction.

Dialogue polish: Getting the rhythm and voice of characters right often required many passes. AI helped us iterate faster.

Consistency checking: With a 3,000+ line screenplay, keeping character voices and plot details consistent is challenging. AI helped catch contradictions.

Format and structure: Proper screenplay formatting is tedious. AI handled the mechanical aspects so we could focus on story.

What Didn't Work

First drafts from scratch: When we tried to have AI generate whole scenes from brief descriptions, the results were generic. The best work came from heavy human direction.

Emotional nuance: The moments that make you feel something ‚Äî a parent's fear, a child's determination ‚Äî required human understanding. AI could help refine these moments but couldn't create them.

Visual humor: Comedy that depends on visual timing and physical comedy needed a human brain that understands how animation works.

The Scene Breakdown

With the screenplay complete, we've also finished our scene breakdown. This document takes each scene and specifies:

  • Location and time of day
  • Characters present
  • Key actions and movements
  • Emotional beats
  • Technical requirements

This breakdown will feed directly into our Blender automation pipeline. Each scene's requirements become inputs for the LLM to generate initial setups.

Characters We're Excited About

We'll do proper character reveals as we finalize designs, but we want to share what makes each one special to us:

Mia: The older sibling who takes charge but learns that leadership means listening, not just directing.

Leo: The younger sibling with a talent for seeing things differently ‚Äî literally the key to solving their biggest challenge.

Ruben: A fairy godfather going through an existential crisis. His depression isn't played for laughs but as a real obstacle he learns to work through.

Jetplane: A dinosaur who became magical in a very unique way. Yes, the color-farting thing. We promise it's funnier in context.

Gabe and Nina: Parents who realize their date night problems pale in comparison to being stranded in the Jurassic period.

What Changes From Here

We're calling this a "complete" first draft, but we know revisions are coming. Some things that might change:

  • Pacing adjustments once we see scenes animated
  • Dialogue tweaks when we hear it spoken
  • Structural changes based on runtime
  • Additions or cuts for clarity

The beauty of our AI-assisted workflow is that these changes can happen faster than in traditional production. Updating the screenplay automatically updates the scene breakdown, which updates the Blender generation prompts.

Next Steps

With the writing phase in a good place, we're moving into:

  1. Character design: Finalizing the look of our cast
  2. Storyboarding: Visualizing key sequences
  3. Pipeline development: Building the Blender automation tools
  4. Asset creation: Environments and props

We'll share progress on each of these as we go. Thanks for following along.
